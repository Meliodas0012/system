CHAPTER 1


INTRODUCTION

I.     Rationale of the Study

      With students' growing interest in pursuing their education at the institution of their choice, although they are distant from where they live. We're developing an Online Enrollment System(OEMS) to bridge this gap. This state of heart system was designed to simplify the enrollment process for students, providing a quicker and more user friendly experience. A simple interface, real time notifications and integration with current student information systems are some of the main features.

II.     Statement of the Problem

      The purpose of this study is to address the issues of aspiring Asian College student who live far away from ACSAT. This study aims to identify the common problems and challenges students encounter when enrolling. The research question that will guide this study are:

1. What is online enrollment management system?

2. What are the challenges of an aspiring Asian College students face when enrolling?

3. How can online management system help ease the challenges of an aspiring Asian College students?

4. What are the benefits of online enrollment management system towards Asian College Students?

5. How does online enrollment management system manage the satisfaction of aspiring Asian College students?

III.    Hypotheses

Null Hypothesis:

1. There is no significant difference in enrollment rates before and after the implementation of an Online Enrollment Management System in Asian colleges.

Alternative Hypotheses:

1. The implementation of an Online Enrollment Management System in Asian colleges will lead to a significant increase in enrollment rates compared to the period before its implementation.

2. Asian colleges implementing a streamlined Online Enrollment Management System will observe a decrease in dropout rates among enrolled students compared to colleges with less efficient enrollment systems.


IV.     Significance of the study

      The significance of this study on online enrollment management systems lies in exploring the experiences and challenges users face during the registration process. Using a non-probability approach, this study aims to uncover valuable insights into the realities of navigating an online registration system. This information will help adjust system functionality and user interface to improve user experience and streamline the enrollment process. Educators and administrators can use the results to develop more effective training programs and support systems for students and staff participating in the use of online registration systems. Additionally, by highlighting the complexities of online enrollment, the study helps improve accessibility and efficiency, thereby ensuring a smoother registration experience for all parties involved. The results of the study will merit the following:

Students . The study would offer the participants a platform which is online enrollment systems to give students with the comfort of enrolling for courses from anywhere with internet access. This flexibility can be particularly beneficial for students with busy schedules or those who live off-campus.


Teachers. The online enrollment system gives teachers real-time access to enrolment data, allowing them to assess student interest in a particular course and plan lessons accordingly. This helps us optimize course delivery and ensure class sizes and facilities are appropriate.

Parents. The online enrollment system allows parents to complete the registration process for their child from the comfort of their home, eliminating the need to visit the school in person. This convenience is especially beneficial for busy parents who have conflicting work schedules or need to enroll multiple children. Online enrollment systems also includes communication features that allow parents to easily communicate with school administrators, teachers, and counselors about registration-related questions and concerns.

Future researchers. The results of the study can serve as an empirical evidence to support or refute hypotheses regarding the implementation and impact of online registration systems. Future researchers can build on this evidence base by conducting additional studies to validate the results, examining additional variables, and question different situations.


V.     Scope and Delimitations of the Study

      The Scope of this system are the prospective students living in various places who are unable to physically visit Asian College for enrollment purposes. It also serves as a platform that gives students access to news and updates about Asian College in real time, allowing them to stay informed about recent changes made to the institution. This is a quantitative system that makes use of sign-up forms for the students to enroll in Asian College. Students from various locations who wish to enroll in Asian College are the target demographic, and the time frame falls within Asian College's enrollment period.
